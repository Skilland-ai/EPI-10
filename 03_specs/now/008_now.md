## Objective
Prepare the execution-ready plan for a 3-hour in-person workshop with EPI10
Salud to define the functional-operational scope of EPI10 Salud MVP 1.0.

The future executor must design the workshop so Raul/equipo can leave the
client session with enough clarity to convert the real client process into
backlog, functional architecture, integrations, automations and deliverables.

Guiding principle:

> Do not design from tools first. Understand and freeze the real operational
> process first; then translate it into states, responsibilities, systems,
> automations and MVP scope.

  offices with Tomas, Carmen and Aitor.

## Scope
In scope:

- Design the workshop as a guided operational design session, not passive
  requirements gathering.
- Separate the work into three audience levels:
  - internal workshop blueprint for Raul/equipo;
  - client-facing pre-workshop package;
  - in-room execution materials.
- Turn the real EPI10 journey into workshop questions, decisions, state
  candidates, responsibility mapping and scope-control mechanisms.
- Prepare a lightweight client-facing email and agenda/preparation sheet that
  can be reviewed quickly and sent the same day.
- Design practical materials for the 3-hour in-person session.
- Keep Fase 1, desirable, Fase 2 and out-of-scope boundaries visible.

Out of scope:

- Do not execute the workshop.
- Do not generate a full Stage 06 execution plan or `06_execution_plan_v1.md`.
- Do not produce a full implementation backlog.
- Do not close final technical architecture, database schema, provider
  contracts, pricing, legal policy or support policy.
- Do not create a new commercial proposal or modify contract, invoice, budget
  or economic conditions.
- Do not promise TellmeGen API integration, automatic genetic ingestion,
  genetic dashboard, autonomous diagnosis or autonomous clinical AI.
- Do not treat Healthie, Odoo or Copilot as already validated beyond existing
  source evidence.
- Do not modify `00_inbox/`, `02_context/`, `04_outputs/spec-driving/`,
  `shared/skills/`, `shared/agents/` or spec 007 outputs.
- Do not commit.

## Work Steps

### 1. Orient and protect scope
Review the inputs and create a short orientation note inside
`blueprint_interno_workshop_scope_operativo_v1.md` covering:

- what is sold/signed versus what is still unknown;
- what Fase 1 is intended to solve;
- what must stay out of Fase 1;
- how TellmeGen, Healthie, Odoo, web/pago and Copilot should be framed;
- risks that must be controlled during the workshop.

Do not reopen commercial scope. Use the signed proposal and CTO diagnosis as
context, not as permission to promise more.

### 2. Ordenar las piezas del sistema EPI10 MVP 1.0
In the internal blueprint, identify and order the main system pieces around the
real client/patient process:

- journey cliente/paciente from interest to final report and follow-up;
- official case/patient states;
- roles and responsibilities;
- web and payment entry;
- Odoo backoffice;
- Healthie or equivalent portal;
- WhatsApp/email and other current communication channels;
- laboratory/TellmeGen manual boundary;
- documents, forms, templates and reports;
- EPI10 Informe Final Copilot;
- automations;
- exceptions and rare cases;
- sensitive data and permission boundaries;
- definition of success for Fase 1.

Level of detail:

- enough to drive the workshop and later convert outputs into backlog,
  functional architecture and integration decisions;
- not enough to freeze final architecture or implement anything.

The executor must distinguish existing facts, assumptions and `Unknown` items.

### 3. Definir que hay que saber/sacar del workshop
For each system piece, define:

- decisions to close during the session;
- information to obtain;
- high-signal questions;
- examples or recent cases to ask for;
- how the answer maps to process, state, owner, data, system, automation or
  validation gate;
- whether the item is Fase 1 imprescindible, Fase 1 deseable, Fase 2 or out of
  scope.

The central framing question is:

> Desde que una persona muestra interes hasta que recibe su informe final y
> seguimiento, que decisiones, datos, documentos, estados, responsables y
> comunicaciones ocurren, y cuales queremos automatizar o controlar en esta
> Fase 1?

Rules:

- Avoid giant questionnaires.
- Avoid filler questions.
- Avoid "preguntar por preguntar".
- Avoid turning the session into a feature wishlist.
- Convert every relevant idea into process, state, owner, data, system,
  automation, human validation or explicit exclusion.

The questions and dynamics must obtain clarity on:

- current real process;
- target MVP process;
- official patient/case states;
- responsible owner by phase;
- source of truth for each data/document;
- what lives in Odoo;
- what lives in Healthie or equivalent portal;
- what lives in web;
- what lives in WhatsApp/email;
- what depends on laboratory/TellmeGen;
- what inputs Copilot needs;
- what can be automated;
- what requires human validation;
- what real exceptions must be handled;
- what is explicitly outside Fase 1.

### 4. Disenar la experiencia presencial de 3 horas
In the internal blueprint, design the in-person session with:

- timeboxed structure;
- work blocks;
- conversation dynamics;
- role of each participant;
- opening script or talking points;
- how to prevent dispersion;
- how to prevent scope creep;
- how to capture decisions;
- how to close the session;
- outputs that must exist at the end.

Use participants deliberately:

- Aitor: source of truth for real process, operational friction and recent
  examples.
- Carmen: source of truth for quality, patient experience and functional/
  professional validation.
- Tomas: source of truth for business success, control, priorities and
  tradeoffs.

The session must feel professional, guided and useful. It must not feel like a
long questionnaire or a feature request collection.

### 5. Crear paquete previo cliente-facing
Create two Markdown files ready for quick Raul review and same-day client send.

#### A. `draft_email_envio_agenda_workshop.md`
Write a brief, professional and warm email in Spanish, ready to copy/paste.

It must explain that Raul/equipo are sharing a simple sheet with the workshop
agenda and a few useful ideas to keep in mind before the in-person session.

Tone requirements:

- no "deberes";
- no administrative burden;
- no pressure to prepare formal documentation;
- light preparation to make the 3 hours more useful;
- close, competent and client-facing.

#### B. `ficha_orden_dia_workshop_epi10.md`
Write a clear, elegant and useful client-facing preparation sheet.

It must include:

- workshop title;
- objective;
- estimated duration: 3 hours;
- expected participants: Tomas, Carmen, Aitor and Skilland/Reboot team;
- agenda by blocks, without excessive technical detail;
- what should be achieved by the end of the session;
- ideas to reflect on before meeting;
- useful materials to have located if available;
- reassuring note: they do not need formal documentation or closed answers.

It should invite them, gently, to think about examples such as:

- how a client/patient reaches EPI10 today;
- steps from first contact to report delivery;
- tasks each person performs today;
- friction, delays or manual work;
- documents, forms, reports or templates currently used;
- what the patient should see or do in the portal;
- what EPI10 needs to control internally;
- what parts of the final report could benefit from Copilot support;
- exceptions or rare cases that usually occur.

Do not write this as an exhaustive questionnaire. Frame it as:

> Os dejamos estas ideas para que, si podeis, vengais con algunos ejemplos o
> casos recientes en mente.
